About

pkgeist is a spooky CLI package manager with a hauntingly good UX.

Meet the Ghosts 👻

Wisp – the energetic greeter 👋

Always the first to pop up and wave, Wisp handles your CLI welcomes and --help flags with ✨mild sarcasm✨.

Byte – the innocent one 🧠

Smol but deadly. Looks sweet but handles all your install commands like a savage. Says things like “Did you mean to break your system? Bold choice.”

Drift – the sleepy background daemon 😴

Drift runs updates quietly in the background and only wakes up to say “I fixed 9 broken packages. You’re welcome. Again.”

Hex – the cursed debugger 😈

Snarky as hell. Finds every dependency issue and roasts your typos: “You typed ‘pakcman’… again. That’s the 3rd time today. Need help?”

The Lore 📜

  • Wisp: Formerly a haunted progress bar. Reincarnated after being stuck animating on a dead install script for 9 years. Still twitchy about it.
  • Byte: Spawned from a cursed floppy disk found in an abandoned CompUSA. Has seen too much. Too pure for this world… unless provoked.
  • Drift: Originated in a forgotten background process on Windows ME. Only speaks in update logs. Loves naps. Hates kernel panics.
  • Hex: Born in the fires of a failed Gentoo compile. Whispers error codes in the dark. Probably knows your root password. Won’t tell you.
